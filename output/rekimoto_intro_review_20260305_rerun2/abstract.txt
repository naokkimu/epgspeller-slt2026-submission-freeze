
Claim: Audited protocol design enables comparable evaluation of EPG silent spelling and shows that layout-aware front ends do not consistently outperform a vector baseline under the tested protocols. Silent speech text entry with electropalatography requires models that generalize across word identities and participants while remaining auditable. We present an evidence-only study of silent spelling from binary palate contact patterns using character-level decoding and lexicon-projected evaluation. We define protocols for word holdout, instance holdout, and cross participant transfer. Using participant labeled datasets and deterministic artifact tracking, we evaluate a vector baseline and two layout aware front ends, and analyze electrode reduction and low shot adaptation. Across our audited runs, the row and column front end tracks the vector baseline more closely than a convolutional grid front end.
