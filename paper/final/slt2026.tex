\title{EPGSpeller: Lexicon-Free Silent Spelling Recognition with Electropalatography}

\author{\IEEEauthorblockN{Anonymous Submission}}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Hands-free character entry motivates decoding beyond fixed command vocabularies; this paper reports an offline recognition-layer study on a corpus-bounded SilentSpeller spelling task, not interactive augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) deployment.
We present EPGSpeller, which decodes character strings from SmartPalate-style electropalatographic contact time series using connectionist temporal classification and greedy decoding before lexicon projection.
On four healthy participants, word-holdout (P1) and instance-holdout (P2) character error rate (CER) stay near 0.18, cross-user transfer (P3) exceeds 0.69, and one held-word example per label on p3 at k=1 partially recovers error to about 0.35 CER.
Results are limited to offline recognition-layer evaluation; interactive AAC input is future work.
\end{abstract}
